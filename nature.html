Nature & Wildlife

Karnataka is a land of incredible biodiversity, blessed with the lush forests of the Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site. These hills are home to a stunning variety of flora and fauna, making it a paradise for nature lovers and wildlife enthusiasts.

From the majestic elephants of Bandipur to the elusive tigers of Nagarhole, the state's national parks and sanctuaries protect some of India's most iconic species. The dense forests, serene rivers, and cascading waterfalls create a vibrant ecosystem that supports countless birds, reptiles, and mammals.

Bandipur National Park

One of India's most famous tiger reserves, known for its large herds of Asiatic elephants.

Jog Falls

One of the highest plunge waterfalls in India, offering a spectacular sight during the monsoon.

Nagarhole National Park

Famous for its high density of tigers, leopards, and the Indian bison (gaur).

Dandeli Wildlife Sanctuary

Diverse wildlife including black panthers, elephants, and hornbills. It is also famous for adventure activities and eco-tourism.

Ranganathittu Bird Sanctuary

A haven for birdwatchers, especially for migratory birds like painted storks, pelicans, and spoonbills. You can also spot marsh crocodiles and otters.

Nandi Hills

Witness stunning sunrise views, enjoy the panoramic vistas, and indulge in activities like trekking and paragliding.
